# Overview

Candoria is a temperate forest world in the Mid Rim, in a system adjacent to one of the early long-distance trade corridors connecting a handful of developing star systems toward the Coreward regions. While it has never been a major hub, occasional travellers, merchants, and scouts pass through the system.

The planet’s continents are dominated by vast forests that stretch for thousands of kilometres. Towering trees form dense canopies that block much of the sunlight from reaching the forest floor, creating dim and cool conditions beneath. The forests themselves are largely unbroken, interrupted only by river valleys, exposed ridges, and the gradual ascent toward mountain ranges, where the terrain opens into broader upland belts.

These highlands marked by rolling upland slopes, elevated valleys, and occasional mesas, provide the only significant open ground suitable for settlement, travel, or agriculture. Both ancient and modern communities have consistently taken advantage of these elevated regions. They offer natural protection from the most dangerous forest predators and provide clear lines of sight across the surrounding wilderness.

Candoria’s rivers, mountains, and highland belts have made settlement and exploration possible, but much of the planet remains largely untamed. The forests continue to encroach on clearings, and vast interior regions are still unknown to those who traverse the early trade corridors. For those who spend time on Candoria, the world feels ancient, and dangerous, but simultaneously peaceful. While system has multiple habitable planets, and is filled with people, the difficulty of mass urbanisation of Candoria maintains it's status as a frontier world, free from the hustle and bustle of some of the other planets in the system.

# Planetary Environment

Candoria’s continents are covered by extensive temperate forests, many of which appear extremely old by planetary standards. Tree growth forms layered canopies that block much of the sunlight from reaching the forest floor, creating cool, dim conditions beneath the upper branches.

Key environmental characteristics include:
- Multi-layered forest canopies reaching hundreds of metres above the ground
- Thick root systems and heavy undergrowth that make ground travel slow
- Large river networks cutting through deep valleys and serving as the most reliable travel routes
- Regular fog banks and rainfall trapped beneath the canopy

Wildlife on Candoria tends toward large herbivores adapted to the dense vegetation, along with predators that rely more on sound and scent than sight within the dim forest interior.

# The Uplands

Across Candoria’s continents, long mountain ranges rise gradually from the surrounding forests, forming wide belts of elevated highland terrain before the peaks themselves begin. Rather than abrupt mesas or isolated plateaus, these regions are characterised by broad upland slopes, broken ridges, and open highland valleys that stretch for hundreds of kilometres along the mountain fronts.

As the forests climb toward these uplands, the ecosystem begins to change.

These highland regions share several characteristics:

- The dense lowland forests thin into shorter woodland and open highland clearings
- Rolling upland ground provides wide, stable surfaces suitable for settlement
- Rocky ridges and foothills create natural defensive terrain
- Higher elevations offer clearer views across the surrounding forests

For travellers and settlers, these 'Uplands' provide the rarest resource on Candoria: open ground that does not need to be constantly reclaimed from the forest.

Because of this, both ancient and modern settlements have tended to form along these elevated belts where the forests begin to give way to highland terrain. Travelling along these routes offers a picturesque view, with towering mountains on one side, and infinite forest on the other, whilst passing through small settlements, farmland, and ending at one of the few larger cities that has developed over the years.

The uplands also provide a practical advantage. Many of Candoria’s most dangerous predators and large fauna dwell within the deep forest environments below. Settlements established along the highland slopes gain a measure of natural protection, as the thinner vegetation and exposed terrain make it more difficult for such creatures to approach unseen.

Scattered across several of these highland regions are weathered remains of older stone structures, suggesting that earlier inhabitants may have recognised the same advantages long before the current age of settlement.

These highlands share several characteristics:
- Wide, level surfaces suitable for habitation
- Reduced forest density compared to the lowland canopy
- Natural cliff faces and defensible approaches
- Clearer views across the surrounding terrain

# Population

In the present era, Candoria remains a lightly settled frontier world.

Population centres are generally small and widely scattered, usually situated within the highland regions where the forest begins to thin near the mountain ranges. These settlements tend to be practical and self-reliant, relying on nearby rivers, fertile soil, and controlled forest clearings for sustenance.

Activity on the planet typically revolves around:
- Modest trade stations supporting overland trade between settlements along one of the several main mountain ranges.
- Some of the more established settlements have begun long-distance trade to the neighbouring system that sits along the main trade network. Due to being slightly set apart, and the lack of hyperdrives in this age, this trade is strictly one way.
- Small agricultural or hunting communities established on the highland slopes

Beyond the inhabited uplands, much of Candoria’s surface remains only loosely explored. The immense forest canopy limits aerial visibility, and large portions of the lowlands have never been surveyed in detail. That said, small communities of forest-dwellers are known to exist, though can be impossible to find, even if you have visited before. These forest-dwellers can often be hostile and fearful of outsiders.
